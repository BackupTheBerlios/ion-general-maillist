So goes life: I never have had a problem with xfs--you
have. The notebook is one and half months old. The desktop
I have too is much older. And it uses xfs as well ... xfs
since I have migrated from reiser4, another very, very
good fs. (I have migrated because of Hans Reiser tragedy
with his wife, you might know about what I am talking;
reiser4 demanded a patch at every kernel release, and the
patches did start to lag behind too much.)

Figure, I have started with reiser3 even with 2.2.x, and
have moved to reiser4 with the first 2.6.x. Brilliant,
superb software!, I virtually never have had problems with
both reiser3 and 4. (Ditto with xfs.)

And believe me, I have unwillingly tested *in vivo*
those fs well. The desktop runs 24/7 ... power outages
permitting [I don't use any UPS longer]. That makes ...?!
one-two power outages a day on the average; with days
having more than ten power outages. (Sumatra belongs also
to the regions in the world where lightnings hit the
most.)

Example: power outage; after two minutes, power is back;
system starts to reboot; after 40 seconds [15 waiting
in LILO] power dies again; after one minute power is
back again; system begins rebooting; 50 seconds later,
peng--next power outage; blackout for one hour; power is
back; all goes well for the next six hours; peng, next
power outage. Etc.

Disagree: xfs, is even more reliable than reiser4 in my
experience. (But I have loved reiser4 very much.)
